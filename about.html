To help people see their donation as a minor gift as opposed to a major sacrifice. Which sounds like a more manageable ask: “a $5 fee” or “a small $5 fee”? Intellectually, we know the fee is the same amount of money, but the second phrase makes the fee sound even more reasonable. In fact, a study by Carnegie Mellon University found that simply adding the word “small” when referring to the shipping fee for a trial DVD increased the response rate by 20 percent.
